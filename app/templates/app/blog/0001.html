Mnemonics

If you know what mnemonics are then skip this section. My writing is no piece of art, so maybe skip it anyway :)

I’ve read several mnemonics books. Many of them feature a memory trick called “visual mnemonics” which leverages the brains ability to remember images to remembering other information. As an example, if you have a list of words: tree, monkey, pizza, building, and fire then you can first picture a tree. Then perhaps the tree transforms into a monkey. The monkey grabs a huge slice of pizza and takes it to the top of a building which spontaneously combusts into fire. It sounds ridiculously silly I know, but it really does work if practised enough. This same method can be extended and is used in memory competitions, but it can also be a fun party trick or even extended to memorizing foreign language vocabulary.

The Application

Mnemonics Trainer generates a list of words for you and recites them to you. You can then memorize them with mnemonics and try to pass the quiz. Afterwards it gives you a score and you can try again. Screen shots:
